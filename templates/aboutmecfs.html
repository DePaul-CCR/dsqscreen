Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS) is a long-lasting disease that affects the body's immune, neurological, and physical systems, causing a range of symptoms. The illness is usually triggered by a viral or flu-like infection. ME affects many different systems in the body, including the central nervous system, immune system, digestive system, and cardiovascular system.

There exist a variety of case definitions to assess ME/CFS, of which three are included in this screener. Because there are no known biomarkers for this illness, it is assessed based on symptoms. The CDC states that the primary symptom of ME/CFS is post-exertional malaise (PEM), which is the worsening of ME/CFS symptoms after minimal physical, mental, or emotional effort. Additional symptoms include muscle weakness, fatigue, disrupted sleep, and either orthostatic intolerance or cognitive dysfunction. Orthostatic intolerance occurs when the autonomic nervous system does not function properly, causing symptoms to worsen when standing or sitting upright. Symptoms of orthostatic intolerance can include fainting, dizziness, weakness, blurred vision, postural orthostatic tachycardia (POTS), reduced heart rate variability at night, and sensitivity to both cold and heat. Other common symptoms of ME/CFS include muscle pain, nerve pain, stiffness in the neck and spine, and sensory issues such as sensitivity to light, sound, touch, skin tingling or numbness, hyperaesthesia (painful skin sensitivity), and allodynia (pain due to a stimulus that does not normally cause pain).
